The opportunity

The futures market rewards simplicity — if you know the rules.

Futures markets — the S&P 500, gold, crude oil — represent the most liquid, actively traded instruments in the world. They run nearly 24 hours a day, offer leverage, and come with tax advantages most retail traders never take advantage of.

The barrier isn't access. It's method. Most traders over-complicate the analysis and end up with a chart that creates paralysis instead of clarity. The trendline method removes everything except what price is actually doing.

The mechanism

Four steps.
Every setup,
every day.

01
Find the valid trendline
Connect two or more significant swing lows (uptrend) or swing highs (downtrend). The line should feel natural — not forced. Three clean contact points = A-tier. Two strong reactions = B-tier. Only take A and B setups in normal market conditions.
02
Wait for the test
When price returns to the trendline, do not enter on contact. Wait and watch. Most trendline entries fail because traders enter too early. The line needs to be tested — then price needs to show its hand.
03
Enter on the confirmation candle
A reversal candle closing away from the trendline — engulfing, pin bar, hammer — is the trigger. Enter on the next candle open. Stop placed just beyond the trendline, not at a round number. Risk: 1–2% of account.
04
Target the next structural level
Target is the last significant high/low, the channel midline, or a measured move — minimum 2:1 risk/reward. Move stop to breakeven at 1:1. Let the trade work. The method does the heavy lifting. Your job is to execute it without deviation.
