The mechanism

Four steps.
Repeat every
New York session.

01
Read market structure on the higher timeframe
Pull up the daily and 4H. Is the market making higher highs and higher lows — or the opposite? Mark the Break of Structure (BOS) and Change of Character (ChoCH) levels. This tells you which direction to look for entries.
02
Identify the liquidity pools
Mark the last significant highs and lows — that's where retail traders are placing stops. When price aggressively sweeps through one of those levels and immediately reverses, that's a liquidity grab. That's the signal.
03
Enter on the order block with confirmation
After the liquidity grab, price retraces into the last institutional order block. Drop to the 15M or 5M and wait for a Break of Structure in your trade direction. That's your entry trigger. 1% risk. Stop below the order block.
04
Let the trade run to the next liquidity pool
Target is the next significant liquidity pool — the level where the opposing stops are resting. Move stop to breakeven at 1:1. Let it run. The market does the work. 2 hours of active analysis, then step away.
